    K’Waun Williams is an American football cornerback for the Denver Broncos of the National Football League (NFL).

    He was born on July 12, 1991, in Paterson, New Jersey, and played college football at the University of Pittsburgh.

    Williams signed with the Cleveland Browns as an undrafted free agent in 2014 and played for them for two seasons before joining the 49ers in 2016. On March 23, 2022, Williams signed a two-year contract with the Denver Broncos.

    He has since become a key player for the Denver Broncos, known for his versatility and ability to play both inside and outside cornerback positions.

    K’Waun Williams Contract

    According to OverTheCap.com, Williams’ deal with the Broncos is actually worth $5.2 million.

    Williams signed for $750,000, which will be distributed over the following two years once the $375,000 wage ceiling is reached in 2022 and 2023.

    Williams will have a $2.105 million cap hit this season due to his $1.34 million basic salary, $510,000 roster bonus, and other compensation.

    Williams’ basic pay will rise to $2.09 million the next year, and his total cap cost will be $2.975 million, including another $510,000 in roster bonuses.
